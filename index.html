Johanna Kivimägi is currently studying electroacoustic composition in Estonian Academy of Music and Theatre in Masters with Malle Maltis. She graduated her bachelor studies in 2015 with teachers Tõnu Kõrvits and Galina Grigorjeva. In 2013-2014 she studied in Iceland Academy of the Arts with Anna Thorvaldsdottir and Pall Ragnar Pallson. She has written music for different groups (choirs, ensembles, solo pieces, electroacoustic pieces etc.) and an important part of her works are collaborations.

Muundumine

Johanna Kivimägi

Muundumine (2017) is a piece for piano and electronics for quadrophonic speaker setting. Premiere 1.06.2017, kohvik 7C, performed by Elle-Riin Volmer (piano) and Johanna Kivimägi (electronics). This recording is a stereo version of the piece.

Piano is played by Elle-Riin Volmer.

Reflection

Johanna Kivimägi

Reflection (2017) is a piece for flute, electronics (5.1 speaker setting) and video. Video is made by Valentin Siltsenko. It is originally written to perform with live flute, electronics (first version was in stereo) and interactive video. There is also an installation version where all the media is fixed (it was made for inSonic2017 festival that took place in december 2017 at ZKM in Karlsruhe). The electronic part is made out of prerecorded flute sounds of the same material as the flute part.
Premiere 11.01.2017 in Tallinn, Sõprus cinema, performed by Gianni Trovalusci (flute). Recorded 9.04.2017 in Tallinn.

Flute - Gianni Trovalusci
Flute on electronics Tarmo Johannes

Sära

Johanna Kivimägi

Sära (2017) is a piece for big ensemble, performed by Sinfonietta, conductor Toomas Vavilov. Premiere 7.04.2017 in Tallinn, Estonian Music Days. This is a recording of the premiere.

Dona nobis pacem

Johanna Kivimägi

Dona nobis pacem (2014) is written for female choir Kammerhääled (conductor Igrid Roose) for their concert tour in summer 2014 in Estonia. This a concert recording from the same tour.
